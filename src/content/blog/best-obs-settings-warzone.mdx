Call of Duty: Warzone is one of the hardest games on the planet to stream cleanly. Its huge open maps, dense foliage, particle-heavy gunfights, and near-constant camera movement create enormous scene complexity that punishes low bitrates and weak encoders.

## Quick Answer

**Stream Warzone at 1080p 60fps with 6,000 Kbps CBR on Twitch (the non-partner cap), using NVENC preset P4–P5 on an NVIDIA GPU. Set in-game Film Grain to 0 — it is the single most impactful setting for stream quality.**

Warzone runs your GPU at 90–100% by itself, so you want a hardware encoder that uses a separate silicon block instead of stealing headroom from the game.

## Why Warzone Is So Hard to Stream

Warzone is compression-unfriendly in almost every way a game can be:

- **Massive open maps** packed with grass, trees, and terrain detail that shimmer at distance.
- **Constant camera movement** from sprinting, driving, and parachuting — this destroys temporal (frame-to-frame) compression efficiency.
- **Particle effects** from explosions, gas, muzzle flash, and smoke that fill the frame with high-frequency detail.
- **High texture fidelity** on weapons and operators that an encoder tries hard to preserve.

The practical result: at 1080p 60fps, Warzone needs the full 6,000 Kbps on Twitch just to look acceptable, and even then a final-circle gas cloud can show blocking. On YouTube Live, where you can push 10,000–12,000 Kbps, the difference is night and day. If you want to understand the tradeoff between sharpness and frame rate, our guide on [30fps vs 60fps streaming](/blog/30fps-vs-60fps-streaming/) breaks it down.

## Recommended OBS Settings by Platform

### Twitch (6,000 Kbps cap)

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080 (drop to 1280×720 if your GPU is struggling)
- **FPS:** 60
- **Encoder:** NVENC (New), H.264
- **Preset:** P4 (Medium) — P5 (Slow) can drop frames because Warzone already saturates the GPU
- **Rate Control:** CBR (Twitch requires it)
- **Bitrate:** 6,000 Kbps
- **Keyframe Interval:** 2 seconds
- **Profile:** High
- **B-frames:** 2

### YouTube Live

- **1080p 60fps:** 10,000–12,000 Kbps
- **1440p 60fps:** 15,000–18,000 Kbps

YouTube's generous ceiling is where Warzone finally breathes. The extra bitrate is spent almost entirely on masking the particle and foliage detail that Twitch's 6,000 Kbps can't hold. See the [YouTube platform guide](/platforms/youtube/) for ingest specifics.

### Kick

Kick allows up to ~8,000 Kbps, so run 7,000 Kbps at 1080p 60fps for a visible bump over Twitch without stressing most home uploads.

## Encoder Choice: NVENC vs x264

For Warzone, **NVENC on any RTX card is the right answer** for 95% of streamers. The game maxes your GPU, but NVENC runs on a dedicated encoder chip, so it adds almost no rendering overhead.

x264 (CPU encoding) only makes sense on a very strong CPU (Ryzen 9 / Core i9) or a dedicated two-PC setup. Even then, you're stuck with the **Faster** or **Fast** preset — Medium will drop frames under Warzone's load. Modern NVENC at P4 matches or beats x264 Fast on quality, so there's little reason to burn CPU cycles. If you want the deeper comparison, read [NVENC vs x264 for streaming](/blog/nvenc-vs-x264-streaming/).

Have an RTX 40-series card and stream to YouTube or Kick? NVENC AV1 is roughly 30% more efficient than H.264 and makes Warzone's busy frames noticeably cleaner. It isn't fully supported on Twitch's main ingest yet, so keep H.264 there.

## In-Game Settings That Change Stream Quality

Warzone is GPU-intensive, so tune to hold frame rate while also making the scene easier to encode:

- **Render Resolution:** 100% — never lower; it looks terrible on stream.
- **Texture Resolution:** Normal or High.
- **Shadow Quality:** Low — biggest FPS gain for the smallest visual cost on stream.
- **Particle Quality:** Low — directly reduces compression complexity in fights.
- **Tessellation:** Off.
- **Anti-Aliasing:** SMAA T2X — clean edges without excessive edge detail.
- **Depth of Field:** Off.
- **Motion Blur:** Off.
- **Film Grain: 0** — the most important setting in this list.

**Why Film Grain matters so much:** film grain adds random noise to every single frame. The encoder can't tell noise from detail, so it wastes precious bitrate encoding meaningless static instead of your gameplay. Turning it to 0 can visibly improve perceived stream quality, especially in the dark, contrast-heavy areas where grain is worst.

## Performance and Audio

To keep your game frame rate stable while encoding:

1. Use **NVENC** so encoding runs off the GPU's dedicated block.
2. Cap in-game FPS at your monitor's refresh rate to avoid frame-time spikes.
3. Use **Game Capture** in OBS, not Display Capture.
4. Disable Windows Game Bar and Game DVR.
5. Trim unneeded browser sources and overlays.

For audio, Warzone's footstep and gunfire cues matter to viewers, so don't crush the game mix:

- **Audio bitrate:** 160 Kbps AAC.
- **Sample rate:** 48 kHz.
- **Mic filters:** Noise Suppression (RNNoise) + Compressor + Limiter at -3 dB.
- Duck game audio only slightly (a few dB) when you talk, rather than muting it.

## FAQ

### Should I stream Warzone at 720p instead of 1080p on Twitch?

If your GPU can't hold 60fps at 1080p while encoding, yes. A stable 720p 60fps stream at 5,000 Kbps looks better than a stuttering 1080p one. Warzone's motion means smooth frame rate beats raw resolution for viewer comfort.

### Why does my Warzone stream look blocky in the gas circle?

Late-game gas and smoke are the worst-case for compression — huge fields of moving semi-transparent particles. At Twitch's 6,000 Kbps cap this is largely unavoidable; lowering in-game Particle Quality and turning off Film Grain reduces it. On YouTube's higher bitrate it mostly disappears.

### Is AV1 worth it for Warzone?

On an RTX 40-series card streaming to YouTube or Kick, yes — AV1's efficiency directly targets the busy, noisy frames Warzone produces. For Twitch, stick with H.264 for now since AV1 isn't on the main ingest.

## Bottom Line

Warzone demands the maximum your platform allows: 6,000 Kbps on Twitch, far more on YouTube, always with NVENC. Kill Film Grain, drop Particle and Shadow quality, and you'll squeeze real quality out of a punishing game.
