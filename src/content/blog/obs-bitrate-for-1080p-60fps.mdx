For OBS 1080p 60fps streaming, the right bitrate depends mostly on platform and upload speed. Twitch users often stay around **6,000 Kbps**, while YouTube streamers can often push **8,000–12,000 Kbps** or more.

## Quick answer table

| Platform | Recommended Bitrate | Safe Upload Speed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Twitch | 6,000 Kbps | 10+ Mbps |
| YouTube | 6,000–12,000 Kbps | 10–18 Mbps |
| Kick | 6,000–8,000 Kbps | 10–12 Mbps |

## Best OBS settings besides bitrate

- **Rate control:** CBR
- **Keyframe interval:** 2
- **Audio bitrate:** 160 Kbps
- **Encoder:** NVENC or x264; AV1 if supported

## Upload speed needed

Use the 1.5x rule for comfort:

- 6,000 Kbps stream → aim for 9–10 Mbps upload
- 10,000 Kbps stream → aim for 15 Mbps upload or more

## Common problems

### Dropped frames

Usually upload speed related.

### Blurry stream

Usually bitrate is too low for 1080p 60fps.
