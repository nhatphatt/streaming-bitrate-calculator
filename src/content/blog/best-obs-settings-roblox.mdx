Roblox is one of the most-streamed platforms on YouTube and Twitch, especially among younger creators. Because Roblox renders relatively simple scenes compared to AAA titles, it compresses cleanly at moderate bitrates — but you still need the right OBS configuration to keep streams smooth and viewer-friendly.

## Why Roblox is forgiving on bitrate

Most Roblox experiences use flat shading, low-poly models, and minimal post-processing. That means less visual entropy per frame, so an encoder can produce a clean image at a lower bitrate than it would for fast-paced AAA games. A typical Roblox stream looks crisp at **4,000–5,000 Kbps at 1080p 60fps** — far below the bitrate a Fortnite or Warzone stream would need at the same settings.

The exceptions are graphics-heavy experiences (Phantom Forces, Doors, Arsenal with full effects) or first-person obby games with rapid camera movement. For these, treat your bitrate budget like a Valorant or Apex Legends stream and bump it 20-30% higher.

## Recommended OBS settings for Roblox

### Twitch (6,000 Kbps cap)

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080 (or 1280×720 for older PCs)
- **FPS:** 60
- **Encoder:** NVENC (New) on NVIDIA GPUs, AMF on AMD, or x264 medium preset
- **Bitrate:** 4,500 Kbps for most experiences, 6,000 Kbps for graphics-heavy games
- **Rate control:** CBR
- **Keyframe interval:** 2 seconds
- **Profile:** High
- **B-frames:** 2

### YouTube Live

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080
- **FPS:** 60
- **Bitrate:** 6,000–9,000 Kbps for sharper edges and text overlay readability
- **Encoder:** NVENC P5 (Slow) or x264 Medium

### Kick

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080
- **FPS:** 60
- **Bitrate:** 7,000 Kbps (Kick allows higher caps than Twitch)
- **Encoder:** NVENC P5 or x264 Medium

## Choosing your capture method

Roblox runs in a windowed or borderless window by default. In OBS:

1. Add a **Game Capture** source.
2. Set Mode to "Capture specific window."
3. Choose `RobloxPlayerBeta.exe` from the dropdown.
4. Enable "Allow transparency" only if you want to overlay scenes — most streamers leave this off.

If Game Capture comes up black (a common Roblox issue), fall back to **Display Capture** and use OBS's window crop to trim out your taskbar.

## Roblox in-game settings that affect stream quality

Several Roblox client settings change how your game compresses on stream:

- **Graphics Quality:** Set to 6-8 for clean visuals; max (10) only if your PC has headroom.
- **Render Quality:** Auto is fine; set manually only if you're frame-pacing manually.
- **Performance Stats:** Off — the FPS overlay creates flickering high-frequency text that wastes bitrate.
- **Camera Mode:** Classic — Follow Camera adds motion smoothing that compresses worse.

For first-person experiences, lower mouse sensitivity slightly. Sudden 180° flicks create motion blur that the encoder will smear regardless of bitrate, especially at 60fps.

## Low-end PC optimizations

Roblox is the favorite gateway for kid streamers, many on entry-level laptops or older desktops. If you can't hold 60fps on stream, drop to **720p 30fps at 3,000 Kbps** before lowering Roblox's graphics. Your viewers will tolerate slightly lower resolution far better than chuggy frame rate.

Other low-end tips:

- Use **NVENC** if you have any NVIDIA GTX 16 series or newer — it's free GPU encoding that doesn't tax your CPU.
- Enable OBS's **Process Priority: Above Normal** under Advanced settings.
- Close Discord overlay, browsers, and capture cards you aren't using.
- Disable Windows Game Bar (`Win+G`) — it conflicts with OBS Game Capture on Roblox.

## Audio for Roblox streams

Roblox audio is heavily loop-based and rarely needs more than 96-128 Kbps AAC:

- **Audio bitrate:** 128 Kbps (default) is plenty.
- **Sample rate:** 48 kHz.
- **Channels:** Stereo.
- Use OBS's **Noise Suppression: RNNoise** filter on your microphone — Roblox audiences are sensitive to background noise during quiet exploration sections.

## Recommended upload speed

For 1080p 60fps Roblox at 4,500 Kbps, you need at least **8 Mbps upload**. For YouTube at 9,000 Kbps, target **15 Mbps**. Many home connections in the US offer 10-25 Mbps upload on cable, which is more than enough for Roblox-quality streams. Use our [Bandwidth Calculator](/tools/bandwidth-calculator/) to verify yours.
