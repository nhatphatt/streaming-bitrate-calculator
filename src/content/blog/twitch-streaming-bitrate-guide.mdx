Choosing the right Twitch bitrate is a balance between video quality, viewer experience, and your upload speed. Push too high and non-partners hit Twitch's limits and lose viewers with slow connections; go too low and fast games turn blurry.

## Quick Answer

**Most Twitch streamers should use 4,500–6,000 Kbps with CBR and a 2-second keyframe interval.** 6,000 Kbps is the practical ceiling for non-partners; partners can reach ~8,000. If your upload is limited, drop resolution before you drop bitrate — a clean 720p60 beats a starved 1080p60.

## What is the max Twitch bitrate?

Twitch officially recommends a video bitrate up to **6,000 Kbps** for non-partners, and its ingest servers will accept up to roughly 8,000 Kbps from partners. But the real constraint isn't just what Twitch accepts — it's [transcoding](/glossary/transcoding/).

When you stream, Twitch ideally creates multiple quality options (source, 720p, 480p, 360p) so viewers on weaker connections can drop down. Transcoding isn't guaranteed for smaller channels, meaning some viewers only get your raw source feed. If that source is 6,000 Kbps at 1080p60, a viewer on mobile data may buffer constantly. That's why pushing the maximum isn't always the best experience.

For most streamers, **6,000 Kbps is the safest practical ceiling**, and many do just as well at 4,500–5,000 Kbps. Compare Twitch's approach to its competitors in [Twitch vs YouTube bitrate](/blog/twitch-vs-youtube-bitrate-comparison/).

## Best Twitch bitrate by resolution

### 720p on Twitch

720p is the most reliable choice on limited upload and often the smartest for competitive play. At 3,500–5,000 Kbps a 720p60 stream looks crisp and stays smooth for almost every viewer. See the [720p streaming bitrate guide](/size/720p-streaming-bitrate/) for exact targets.

### 1080p on Twitch

1080p is the popular target. For 30fps, 4,500–6,000 Kbps looks excellent. For 60fps, 6,000 Kbps is the standard — max out the bitrate because 60fps needs the extra data to stay sharp in motion. Details in the [1080p streaming bitrate guide](/size/1080p-streaming-bitrate/).

### 1440p on Twitch

Twitch doesn't officially support 1440p, and the 6,000 Kbps recommendation is really too low to do 1440p justice. You can force higher bitrates, but you'll likely hit ingest limits and viewer buffering. For most creators, 1080p is the better ceiling on Twitch.

## Best Twitch bitrate by use case

- **Competitive gaming:** 720p 60fps or 1080p 60fps — prioritize frame rate for fast motion.
- **Just Chatting / IRL:** 720p or 1080p at 30fps often looks cleaner since motion is slow.
- **Limited upload speed:** 720p is almost always the smarter choice; it reaches more viewers.
- **Retro / pixel-art games:** lower resolutions with a high keyframe frequency handle sharp edges well.

## Bitrate vs viewer experience

Twitch streamers often forget that the highest source quality isn't automatically the best experience. A viewer's playback is only as good as their connection allows, and without guaranteed transcoding, a heavy 6,000 Kbps 1080p60 source can lock out anyone on mobile data or slow home internet. They either buffer or leave.

The practical sweet spot balances three things:

- **Encoder efficiency:** a good x264 preset or NVENC squeezes more quality out of the same bitrate.
- **Scene complexity:** particle-heavy shooters need more bitrate than slow strategy games.
- **Audience:** if your viewers are largely on phones, a lighter 720p source reaches more of them.

When in doubt, favor a stable, slightly lower bitrate that everyone can watch over a maxed-out source that only some viewers see cleanly.

## Best OBS settings for Twitch

- **Rate control:** [CBR](/glossary/cbr/) — Twitch requires constant bitrate.
- **Bitrate:** 4,500–6,000 Kbps.
- **Keyframe interval:** 2 seconds.
- **Encoder:** NVENC or x264 (RTX 40/50-series can try AV1 as Twitch rolls out support).
- **Preset:** x264 "veryfast," or NVENC "Quality."
- **Audio bitrate:** 160 Kbps AAC.
- **Profile:** high.

Twitch mandates CBR because it keeps your outgoing data steady, which protects the ingest server and reduces buffering. ## Upload speed and stability

Keep your total stream data under about 70–75% of your real upload speed. A 6,000 Kbps stream plus 160 Kbps audio needs roughly 8–10 Mbps of stable, wired upload. If you see dropped frames, lower the bitrate first, then check for Wi-Fi or background uploads. ## Frequently asked questions

### What is the best bitrate for Twitch streaming?

For most streamers, 4,500–6,000 Kbps depending on resolution and frame rate. 720p60 does well around 4,000–5,000 Kbps; 1080p60 should sit at 6,000 Kbps.

### Is 6,000 Kbps enough for 1080p 60fps on Twitch?

Yes — 6,000 Kbps is the standard, recommended target for 1080p60 and the practical ceiling for non-partners. It's plenty for the vast majority of games.

### Should I stream 720p or 1080p on Twitch?

If your upload speed is limited or inconsistent, 720p gives a better overall viewer experience because it's easier for weak connections and more likely to reach everyone smoothly. Choose 1080p only when you can reliably feed it 6,000 Kbps.

### Does Twitch transcode my stream?

Not always. Transcoding (creating lower-quality options for viewers) is guaranteed for partners and rolled out inconsistently for smaller channels, which is another reason to avoid maxing out your source bitrate.
