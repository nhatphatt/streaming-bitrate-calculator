Marvel Rivals became one of the most-watched 6v6 hero shooters on Twitch and YouTube almost overnight. Its colorful, particle-heavy combat puts real pressure on encoders — bright explosions, stacked ability effects, and rapid camera turns all reduce compression efficiency. Here's how to dial OBS in so your stream stays readable even at the bitrate caps.

## Quick Answer

**Stream Marvel Rivals at 1080p 60fps with 6,000 Kbps CBR on Twitch using NVENC preset P5. If you have Twitch Enhanced Broadcasting or stream to YouTube/Kick, switch to HEVC and push 8,000+ Kbps — the efficiency gain visibly cleans up teamfights.**

Marvel Rivals is one of the games where paying for a higher-quality encoder preset genuinely pays off in image clarity.

## Why Marvel Rivals Stresses Your Encoder

Three things make it harder to stream than most hero shooters:

1. **Particle density** — Iron Man, Storm, Magneto, and Scarlet Witch generate stacked effects that fill the screen with high-frequency detail.
2. **Camera shake and screen warps** — ultimates like Doctor Strange's portal and Hulk's smashes introduce motion the encoder can't predict.
3. **Vertical movement** — flying heroes cause constant vertical pans that wreck temporal compression.

The practical result: at the same Twitch 6,000 Kbps cap, Marvel Rivals looks noticeably softer than Valorant or CS2. To compensate, use the highest-quality encoder preset your hardware can sustain. ## Recommended OBS Settings by Platform

### Twitch (6,000 Kbps cap, standard broadcasting)

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080
- **FPS:** 60
- **Encoder:** NVENC H.264 (New)
- **Preset:** P5 (Slow) — drop to P4 only if your GPU is overloaded
- **Bitrate:** 6,000 Kbps
- **Rate Control:** CBR
- **Keyframe Interval:** 2 seconds
- **Profile:** High
- **Psycho Visual Tuning:** On
- **B-frames:** 2

### Twitch Enhanced Broadcasting (multitrack / HEVC)

If you have access to Enhanced Broadcasting, switch to multitrack with HEVC and push **8,000–10,000 Kbps**. The HEVC efficiency gain alone makes teamfights meaningfully cleaner. See the [Twitch platform page](/platforms/twitch/) for eligibility.

### YouTube Live

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080 or 2560×1440
- **FPS:** 60
- **Bitrate:** 9,000–12,000 Kbps (1080p) or 14,000–18,000 Kbps (1440p)
- **Encoder:** NVENC P5/P4, or NVENC AV1 on RTX 40-series for the cleanest result

### Kick

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080
- **FPS:** 60
- **Bitrate:** 8,000 Kbps (a safe sweet spot on Kick's higher cap)
- **Encoder:** NVENC P5

## In-Game Graphics That Affect Stream Quality

Marvel Rivals' Lumen-based lighting and dynamic shadows are gorgeous but expensive. Tune for stream readability over photorealism:

- **Graphics Quality preset:** Medium or High — Ultra/Cinematic offer diminishing returns at 1080p output.
- **Anti-Aliasing:** TSR (Temporal Super Resolution) — a clean edge profile that compresses well.
- **Motion Blur:** Off, always — it hides ability tells and smears encoded footage.
- **Bloom and Lens Flare:** Medium — maxed bloom adds high-frequency noise that eats bitrate.
- **Shadows:** High — Low/Medium shadows shimmer at distance and waste bitrate.
- **DLSS / FSR:** Quality mode on RTX 30/40 or RDNA3 GPUs to cut GPU load with minimal visual cost.

| GTX 16-series | NVENC H.264 Quality — older NVENC, still beats x264 Fast |

For most streamers, **NVENC on a dedicated RTX card is the clear winner** — it leaves the CPU free for game logic, Discord, and browser sources. If you're weighing brands, our [AMD AMF vs NVENC](/blog/amd-amf-vs-nvenc-encoding/) comparison goes deeper.

## Two-PC Setup Considerations

If your in-game frame rate dips in late-game teamfights on a single PC, a dedicated streaming PC plus capture card removes the contention. The capture PC handles all encoding; the gaming PC focuses on rendering. With a consumer capture card you can capture 1440p144 from your gaming rig and re-encode to 1080p60 for stream — getting both performance and quality. ## Audio Recommendations

Marvel Rivals' SFX layer is dense and important — viewers rely on audio cues for ability timings. Don't bury the game under your voice:

- **Game audio:** duck a few dB when you talk; use a mic compressor instead of aggressive ducking.
- **Mic bitrate:** 128 Kbps minimum, 160 Kbps for higher fidelity.
- **Mic filters:** RNNoise + Compressor + Limiter at -3 dB.
- **Sample rate:** 48 kHz to match the game audio engine.

## Recommended Upload Speed

For 1080p 60fps at 6,000 Kbps on Twitch, you need at least **10 Mbps upload**. For YouTube at 9,000 Kbps, target **15 Mbps**. Cable connections in major US metros typically deliver 20–50 Mbps upload — comfortable for any setting in the table below.

## FAQ

### Why does Marvel Rivals look softer than my Valorant streams at the same bitrate?

Valorant has flat art and few particles; Marvel Rivals fills the frame with layered ability effects and vertical camera motion. Both are worst cases for compression, so at a fixed 6,000 Kbps Rivals simply has more detail to spread the same bits across. HEVC or higher bitrate is the fix.

### Is HEVC worth switching to for Marvel Rivals?

Yes, where it's supported (YouTube, Kick, Twitch Enhanced Broadcasting). HEVC is roughly 30–40% more efficient than H.264, and Rivals' busy scenes are exactly where that efficiency shows.

### Should I stream at 1440p?

Only on YouTube with a strong upload and GPU. On Twitch, 1080p at the 6,000 Kbps cap looks better than a starved 1440p feed — there simply isn't enough bitrate to feed the extra pixels.

## Bottom Line

Marvel Rivals rewards the best encoder you can run. Max Twitch's 6,000 Kbps with NVENC P5, move to HEVC on Enhanced Broadcasting or YouTube, kill motion blur, and keep shadows high.
