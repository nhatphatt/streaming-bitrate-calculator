Palworld remains one of the most-streamed survival-craft games on Twitch and YouTube. Its open world, dense foliage, and frequent base-vs-raid combat create unique encoder challenges — and its Unreal Engine 5 tech is genuinely heavy on hardware. Get OBS configured correctly and viewers will see crisp creature animations and base details even at modest Twitch bitrates.

## Quick Answer

**Stream Palworld at 1080p 60fps with 6,000 Kbps CBR on Twitch using NVENC preset P5, Psycho Visual Tuning on. Put the game on an NVMe SSD — chunk-loading hitches from an HDD are the biggest cause of visible stutter for Palworld streamers.**

Palworld is both GPU-heavy (Lumen/Nanite) and storage-sensitive, so this game rewards attention to both your encoder and your disk.

## Why Palworld Is Harder to Stream Than It Looks

On the surface Palworld is a stylized open-world game. In practice, three streaming pain points show up:

1. **Foliage density** — grass and tree detail at distance produces shimmer that compresses poorly.
2. **Camera-relative motion** — you're constantly running, gliding, or flying, keeping temporal compression working hard.
3. **Busy bases** — 10+ Pals working create independent character animations the encoder treats as many separate motion vectors.

The combination means Palworld benefits more than most survival games from a higher-quality encoder preset. For context on how motion affects bitrate needs, see our note on [30fps vs 60fps streaming](/blog/30fps-vs-60fps-streaming/).

## Recommended OBS Settings by Platform

### Twitch (6,000 Kbps cap)

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080
- **FPS:** 60 (or 30 if you can't hold 60 stable in big bases)
- **Encoder:** NVENC (New) — H.264 for standard Twitch, HEVC for Enhanced Broadcasting
- **Preset:** P5 (Slow) for cleanest output
- **Bitrate:** 6,000 Kbps for 60fps, 4,500 Kbps for 30fps
- **Rate Control:** CBR
- **Keyframe Interval:** 2 seconds
- **B-frames:** 2
- **Profile:** High
- **Psycho Visual Tuning:** On (helps clean up foliage shimmer)

### YouTube Live

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080 or 2560×1440 (if upload supports it)
- **FPS:** 60
- **Bitrate:** 9,000–12,000 Kbps (1080p) or 16,000 Kbps (1440p)
- **Encoder:** NVENC P5, or NVENC AV1 on RTX 40-series for foliage-heavy scenes

### Kick

- **Resolution:** 1920×1080
- **FPS:** 60
- **Bitrate:** 8,000 Kbps
- **Encoder:** NVENC P5

## In-Game Graphics Tuning for Clean Stream Output

Palworld runs on Unreal Engine 5 with Lumen and Nanite. These look great but are GPU-hungry. Tune for stream output, not screenshots:

- **Graphics preset:** Epic for VOD recordings, High for live streaming if your GPU is borderline.
- **View Distance:** Far, not Epic — Epic adds shimmering distant foliage that wastes encoder bits.
- **Shadows:** High — Epic has noisy contact shadows that compress badly.
- **Anti-Aliasing:** TSR Quality — a softer but cleaner image for streaming than DLSS Performance.
- **Motion Blur:** Off — important for first-person aiming clarity.
- **Foliage Quality:** High, not Epic — small visual gain, large bitrate cost.
- **Upscaling (DLSS/FSR/TSR):** Quality mode is fine; never Performance mode for streaming — it adds artifacts the encoder amplifies.

## Encoder Choice for Palworld

NVENC HEVC is the clear winner where your platform supports it (YouTube, Kick, Twitch Enhanced Broadcasting). Standard Twitch users on H.264 should still prefer NVENC over x264 — the GPU encoder leaves your CPU free for Palworld's heavy AI and world-simulation loop.

| Hardware | Recommended encoder |
|----------|---------------------|
| RTX 40-series | NVENC HEVC or AV1 P5 |
| RTX 30-series | NVENC H.264 P5 |
| RTX 20-series | NVENC H.264 P4 |
| RX 7000-series | AMF HEVC High Quality |
| Ryzen CPU only | x264 Fast (Medium drops frames in big bases) |

Comparing codecs? Our [H.264 vs H.265 vs AV1](/blog/h264-vs-h265-vs-av1/) breakdown explains why HEVC's edge handling matters more in foliage-heavy games.

## Avoiding Chunk-Loading Hitches on Stream

Palworld streams chunks of the open world from disk as you traverse. On HDD storage this causes visible hitching every few seconds — viewers see stutter that no bitrate setting can fix. Two solutions:

1. **Move the game to an NVMe SSD.** This is the #1 quality-of-life upgrade for Palworld streamers in 2026 — sub-millisecond read latency eliminates the visible hitches.
2. **Enable "Process Priority: Above Normal"** in OBS Advanced settings so OBS keeps encoding smoothly through OS-level disk pauses.

If you can't move to SSD, lower stream FPS to 30. The encoder uses the extra per-frame time to mask hitches better. If you're chasing stutter, our guide on [fixing stream lag and stutter](/blog/how-to-fix-stream-lag-and-stutter/) has more.

## Recording vs Streaming

Many Palworld creators stream live to Twitch and upload edited highlights to YouTube. To preserve headroom while doing both:

- Use OBS's separate **Recording output** at a higher bitrate (20–30 Mbps H.264 or 12–15 Mbps HEVC).
- Set Recording resolution to 1440p or 2160p if your GPU can handle it.
- Keep the stream output at 1080p 60fps for live viewers.

This dual-output flow gives you a high-quality mezzanine file for editing without inflating your live stream cost. Long recording sessions can produce 20+ GB per hour at higher recording bitrates, so plan ahead with the [recording time calculator](/tools/recording-time-calculator/).

## Audio for Palworld Streams

Palworld has surprisingly good ambient and combat audio. Don't smother it:

- **Mic bitrate:** 128 Kbps minimum.
- **Game audio:** keep at full level; duck only when explaining mechanics.
- **Mic filters:** RNNoise + Compressor + Limiter at -3 dB.
- **Sample rate:** 48 kHz.

## Recommended Upload Speed

For Twitch 1080p 60fps at 6,000 Kbps, you need at least **10 Mbps upload**. For YouTube at 9,000 Kbps, plan for **15 Mbps**. On 5G home internet or rural DSL, drop to 720p 30fps at 3,000 Kbps.

## FAQ

### Why does my Palworld stream stutter every few seconds?

That's almost always chunk-loading from a slow disk, not your encoder or bitrate. Move the game to an NVMe SSD to fix it. Setting OBS process priority to Above Normal helps OBS ride through the disk pauses.

### Should I lower foliage settings for a cleaner stream?

Yes — set Foliage Quality and View Distance to High/Far rather than Epic. Distant foliage shimmer is one of the biggest bitrate wasters in Palworld and the visual loss on stream is minimal.

### Can I record at higher quality than I stream?

Absolutely. Use OBS's separate Recording output at 20–30 Mbps and 1440p while streaming at 1080p 60fps. That gives you a clean file for YouTube edits without raising your live encoding load.

## Bottom Line

Palworld is a two-front challenge: a heavy UE5 renderer and disk-dependent world streaming. Max Twitch's 6,000 Kbps with NVENC P5, keep foliage and view distance at High/Far, and above all put the game on an SSD.
