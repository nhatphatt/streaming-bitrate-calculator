For Delta Force, use **6,000 Kbps CBR at 1080p 60fps** on Twitch with NVENC preset P5, or **10,000–12,000 Kbps** on YouTube Live for noticeably sharper results. The game's mix of dense outdoor environments, smoke grenades, destruction effects, and fast extraction sequences pushes encoders hard — getting your settings right before going live makes the difference between a watchable stream and a blurry mess during firefights.

## Why Delta Force Is Demanding to Stream

Delta Force combines the visual complexity of a large-scale tactical shooter with the chaotic action of an extraction game. Several of its characteristics work directly against your encoder:

- **Dense foliage and terrain detail** across outdoor maps creates constant background motion
- **Smoke and gas effects** during breaches and grenade throws spike bitrate demands sharply
- **Destruction and debris** from explosive gameplay generate high-entropy frames
- **Fast player movement** during extractions and vehicle sequences leaves the encoder little time to stabilize

At Twitch's 6,000 Kbps ceiling, you are at the edge of what H.264 can do cleanly for this content. Every setting choice below is aimed at maximizing the quality you can squeeze out of that budget.

## Recommended OBS Settings by Platform

### Twitch

- **1080p 60fps:** 6,000 Kbps, NVENC H.264 (New), Preset P5
- **720p 60fps:** 4,500–5,000 Kbps — use this if GPU load exceeds 95% while streaming
- **Encoder:** NVENC H.264 for single-PC setups; x264 Fast/Faster only on a dedicated streaming PC

### YouTube Live

- **1080p 60fps:** 10,000–12,000 Kbps — a clear step up in clarity over Twitch
- **1440p 60fps:** 15,000–18,000 Kbps — the sweet spot for high-detail extraction footage
- YouTube's server-side transcoding means every viewer tier benefits, so push bitrate higher when your upload allows

### Kick

- **1080p 60fps:** 6,500–8,000 Kbps — Kick's higher cap is worth using in full for a game this complex
- **720p 60fps:** 4,500–5,500 Kbps for bandwidth-limited setups

Use the [Bandwidth Calculator](/tools/bandwidth-calculator/) to confirm your upload speed can support these targets before going live. Your stream bitrate should not exceed 75% of your sustained upload speed.

## NVENC Encoder Settings (Recommended for Most Streamers)

Delta Force is GPU-intensive. NVENC offloads encoding to a dedicated chip on your GPU, leaving the shader cores free for the game itself. These are the full settings to enter in OBS under **Settings → Output → Advanced → Streaming**:

- **Encoder:** NVIDIA NVENC H.264 (new)
- **Rate Control:** CBR
- **Bitrate:** (see table above per platform)
- **Keyframe Interval:** 2 seconds
- **Preset:** P5 (Slow) — drop to P4 if you see GPU-related frame drops
- **Tuning:** High Quality
- **Multipass Mode:** Two Passes (Quarter Resolution)
- **Profile:** High
- **B-frames:** 2

**RTX 40-series users:** NVENC AV1 is worth testing for YouTube and Kick, where higher bitrate headroom already exists. AV1 at 10,000 Kbps beats H.264 at 12,000 Kbps for extraction-game content. Check our [H.264 vs H.265 vs AV1 comparison](/blog/h264-vs-h265-vs-av1/) for a detailed breakdown of the quality differences.

## x264 Encoder Settings (Dedicated Streaming PC Only)

If you run a two-PC setup with a dedicated encoder machine, x264 CPU encoding at a slower preset beats NVENC at the same bitrate:

- **Preset:** Fast (single powerful CPU) or Veryfast (lower-end CPU)
- **Rate Control:** CBR
- **Bitrate:** Match the per-platform values above
- **Profile:** High
- **Keyframe Interval:** 2

Do not attempt x264 Medium or Slow on a single PC running Delta Force — the CPU cost will cause in-game frame drops even on a Ryzen 9 or Core i9. For single-PC setups, NVENC is the correct choice every time. See our [NVENC vs x264 guide](/blog/nvenc-vs-x264-streaming/) for the full performance and quality tradeoff analysis.

## OBS General Settings

Beyond the encoder tab, these settings matter for competitive gameplay streaming:

**Video Settings (Settings → Video):**
- **Base (Canvas) Resolution:** 1920×1080
- **Output (Scaled) Resolution:** 1920×1080 (no downscale for 1080p streaming)
- **Downscale Filter:** Lanczos (if downscaling to 720p)
- **Common FPS Values:** 60

**Output Mode:** Always use **Advanced**, not Simple. Advanced mode unlocks the full NVENC parameter set and lets you run different settings for streaming and recording simultaneously.

**Process Priority:** Keep OBS at Normal priority. Setting it to High can cause microstutter in Delta Force by starving game threads.

## In-Game Settings for Streaming

Delta Force's settings menu has several options that directly impact both your frame rate and stream quality. These are the adjustments that matter most:

- **Film Grain / Noise:** Set to **0 or Off** — this is the single highest-impact setting. Film grain adds random per-frame noise that the encoder treats as real detail and wastes bitrate on. Removing it can visibly improve stream clarity at no in-game quality cost.
- **Motion Blur:** **Off** — blur effects interact poorly with temporal compression and reduce sharpness of fast movement on stream
- **Ambient Occlusion:** Low or Off — AO is nearly invisible on a compressed stream but costs GPU performance
- **Shadow Quality:** Medium — shadows are visible on stream; Low looks noticeably flat
- **Texture Quality:** High — textures survive compression well and matter for viewer experience
- **Particle Effects:** Medium — reducing from Ultra cuts encoder complexity during smoke-heavy firefights
- **Anti-Aliasing:** SMAA or TAA at medium quality — reduces jagged edges that compress poorly
- **Render Resolution / Resolution Scale:** 100% — never go below native; upscaling artifacts are visible on stream

Cap your in-game frame rate at 120–144 fps (or your monitor's refresh rate). Uncapped frames push GPU temperatures up and can cause NVENC quality drops due to thermal throttling.

## Recording While Streaming Delta Force

If you want a high-quality local recording alongside your stream, use OBS Advanced Output mode to set independent recording settings:

- **Recording Encoder:** NVENC HEVC (H.265) or NVENC AV1 (RTX 40-series)
- **Rate Control:** CQP
- **CQP Level:** 20 (HEVC) or 22 (AV1 — smaller files, same perceived quality)
- **Container:** MKV (remux to MP4 after via File → Remux Recordings)

A one-hour Delta Force recording at 1080p 60fps HEVC CQP 20 typically runs **4–7 GB**, depending on map and action density. Use the [Recording Time Calculator](/tools/recording-time-calculator/) to plan your storage before a long session.

For the full recording configuration, the [OBS settings hub](/obs/) covers every parameter in detail.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

**Dropped frames during extraction sequences:** Lower NVENC preset from P5 to P4. Extraction lobbies involve heavy geometry streaming and can spike GPU load briefly.

**Pixelation during smoke grenades:** Smoke is high-entropy content that overwhelms fixed bitrate headroom. On Twitch, this is unavoidable at 6,000 Kbps. On YouTube, bump to 12,000 Kbps. In-game, set Particle Effects to Medium to reduce smoke density slightly.

**Stream looks blurry compared to the game:** Check that your OBS canvas and output resolution both match your game resolution. A mismatch causes unintended downscaling. Also verify you are using the Lanczos filter if any scaling is active.

**High CPU with x264:** Switch to NVENC. Delta Force is CPU-intensive due to its physics and destruction simulation — there is no spare headroom for software encoding on most single-PC setups.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What bitrate should I use for Delta Force on Twitch?

Use **6,000 Kbps CBR** — that is Twitch's practical maximum for most streamers and the highest quality you can push to viewers reliably. If you have Twitch Partner status with transcoding, 6,000 Kbps at 1080p 60fps with NVENC P5 will look sharp during normal gameplay. Smoke-heavy moments will still compress less cleanly; that is a platform limit, not an OBS configuration issue.

### Should I stream Delta Force at 1080p or 720p?

If your GPU can handle it without dropping below 90fps in-game, stream at **1080p 60fps**. Delta Force has enough environmental detail that the 720p downscale loses readable information during ranged engagements. Drop to 720p 60fps at 4,500–5,000 Kbps only if you are consistently seeing GPU-related frame drops in OBS stats.

### Does NVENC AV1 help for Delta Force streaming?

Yes, on RTX 40-series GPUs streaming to YouTube or Kick. AV1 at 10,000 Kbps produces noticeably sharper smoke and foliage detail than H.264 at the same bitrate. Twitch's AV1 support exists but transcoding availability varies — test it, but keep an H.264 profile as a fallback. Read the full [codec comparison](/compare/) to understand the quality and compatibility tradeoffs.

### What is the best OBS scene setup for competitive extraction gameplay?

Use a single **Game Capture** source set to capture Delta Force specifically — not Display Capture or Window Capture. Game Capture uses the lowest-latency path and imposes the smallest GPU overhead. Disable all OBS filters on the source (color correction, sharpening) during competitive play; they add processing overhead and can cause frame timing issues at 60fps.
